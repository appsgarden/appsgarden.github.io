macOS

Apps I use everyday

Safari

For macOS, it's the best browser you can have. Its optimization allows you to consume little and is fast and reliable.

#Browsers

Firefox

Great alternative for those who don't want to use the default browser. I love its philosophy and its cross-platform for any system.

#Browsers

Telegram

I'm sure many prefer to use Signal for privacy or Whatsapp for not having it at all. I was lucky enough to take all my friends from Whatsapp to Telegram years ago and asking them to switch to Signal is too much. Telegram is excellent because it contains channels and groups for many passions and information.

#Communication

Facetime

With telegram video calls I use Facetime less often, but it's a valid alternative for those who have the entire Apple ecosystem.

#Communication

Microsoft Teams & Slack

Great for work and study. The only problem, both for Team and Slack, is a bad optimization for macOS devices, even for simple operations they can bring the fan of your device to maximum.

#Communication

Apple music

For Apple Watch owners this is the best choice. When I play sports, I avoid taking my phone with me. I've noticed that the music recommending algorithm has improved a lot lately, but still behind that of Spotify. Furthermore, lossless and Dolby Atmos have recently been added without any price changes.

#Creative

Pixelmator Pro

One of the few products that allow a single purchase without having to subscribe. I'm not a photographer so my use is pretty basic, but Pixelmator contains a multitude of features to be able to make so many changes to photos. I've also seen Affinity Photo but in my case, the Pixelmator interface is much simpler.

#Creative

Elmedia Video Player

More refined interface than VLC and complete support for devices with AirPlay, Chromecast, and DLNA.

#Media

HandBrake

Open-source video transcoder, never given any problems.

#Media

Adapter

Converts Video, Audio, and Images.

#Media

Axure RP 9

Fantastic software for creating and modeling prototypes. It allows the import of patterns and external libraries and the possibility to share your prototypes online.

#Productivity

PDF Expert

Another product without any subscription to subscribe. It allows you to manage PDFs quickly and easily. Unfortunately, the iOS app is less curated and paid with a subscription.

#Productivity

Homebrew

Best package Manager for macOS.

#Productivity

KeePassXC

Open source and totally offline password manager. In my case, I use it to save backup codes and important documents that I want to keep separate from iCloud Keychain.

#Security

ProtonVPN

Fast and easy VPN. For those who are meticulous about finding a VPN that guarantees great privacy, this is one of the choices along with Mullvad.

#Security

Suspicious Package

It allows you to analyze in detail all the .pkg packages before installing them. You can get information such as the files the package installs, running scripts, and more.

#Security

Little Snitch

The best firewall you can install. It allows you to block and create rules for any application and service on the mac.

#Security

TimeMachine

It does everything, you don't have to do anything.

#Backup

Carbon Copy Cloner

Good backup manager with many features and in particular the possibility of checksum checks on the files being backed up.

#Backup

Borg

For offsite backups. Make encrypted backups on borgbase. Used for daily backups of essential documents only.

#Backup

AdGuard

Blocking and privacy protection software for blocking pop-ups, banners, and web tracking, malware and phishing. My advice is to wait for offers and discounts that allow you to purchase the lifetime license for a few dollars.

#Utilities

coconutBattery

Detailed information on the battery of your devices such as MacBook and iPhone.

#Utilities

DaisyDisk

Check which files are taking up space inside your mac. Very simple and nice interface. Unlike other products, DaisyDisk allows you to go into the 'depth' of each Mac path.

#Utilities

JDownloader2

Download any file with a simple webpage link.

#Utilities

ClipGrab

Download videos from youtube in various audio/video formats.

#Utilities

Keka

Zipping and unzipping any file. Supports multiple formats.

#Utilities

qBittorrent

Download and manage torrent files.

#Utilities

Audacity

Complete and hassle-free handling of audio files.

#Utilities

Magnet

Move and resize windows in macOS using keyboard shortcuts or snap areas.

#Utilities

MusicHarbor

To manage the artist library I follow on Apple Music. With MusicHarbor I get the whole list of all the new releases of the various artists that I can add to the library.

#Utilities

KeyboardCleanTool

Makes the macbook keyboard unusable so that it can be cleaned without accidentally pressing any keys.

#Utilities

Alfred

Spotlight is a great way to search for anything. But many times it's slow, it doesn't show all the files and slowly. So you realize that it's limited. Alfred is a great replacement for Spotlight, light and fast. It's available in a free version but buying a license opens up a world of possible search combinations that you can do. Through the Workflows, I can research on Reddit, view my repo on GitHub, search for torrents and many other things. It has tons of features and once configured properly, I would just use it for anything.

#Utilities

Reeder 5

Fantastic RSS feed manager. Available for both macOS and iOS. The latest update allows the synchronization of your feed via iCloud.

#Utilities

VMware Fusion

You can get a free license to use it for personal and non-commercial purposes. Excellent emulation and simple settings.

#Utilities

Kindle

For those who have a Kindle, it is an excellent app to be able to read their books and comics even on mac.

#Utilities

AppCleaner

AppCleaner allows you to uninstall any application on the Mac by deleting all files and folders that are related to the application itself. Many times, in fact, moving an application to the trash will not delete all the files and in the long run the Mac will have mountains of totally useless files of applications that you no longer use.

#Utilities

DevCleaner

If you use Xcode it allows you to see all the files downloaded for emulation and more. For example, many times there are installations of old iOS versions that can be deleted.

#Utilities

Amphetamine

It allows you to keep your Mac active without going to sleep. It has a lot of customization and is completely free.

#Utilities

Sublime Text

Fantastic text editor and IDE. Behind it is an entire community that works to form packages to customize, improve, add features and meet your every need. From simple text writing to code versioning, Sublime Text is the best.

#Coding

Jetbrains suite

The Jetbrains suite is immense. Each product is built for a specific language. There're already numerous settings and supporting software to work with your favorite programming language. On the other hand, they consume much more resources than another simpler and more reactive ide.

#Coding

Xcode

For software development that must run on Apple devices, Xcode is the only one.

#Coding

Steam

Let's say that if you go for gaming, perhaps it's not worth taking a mac. Many games emulate them with VMware Fusion or via Boot Camp. However, I have to admit that many of the games I like are compatible with macOS.

#Gaming

DOSBox

If you like old DOS games, this is the best emulator you can find.

#Gaming

OpenEmu

Open-source emulator, supports many consoles such as: Atari, Nintendo, Sega, PSP, GameBoy and many others.

#Gaming

Suite Apple (Keynote, Numbers, Pages)

It's like Microsoft's suite. It has Office compatibility and handles even complex spreadsheets well. Tip: if you rarely use Office, uninstall it from your mac and use the web browser version, you save space and the infinity of installed Microsoft services.

#Writing

Apple Note

If you are in the Apple ecosystem it is a must have, both for fast and articulate notes.

#Writing
